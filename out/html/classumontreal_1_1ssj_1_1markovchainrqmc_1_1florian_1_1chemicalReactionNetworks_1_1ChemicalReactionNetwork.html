Detailed Description

Implements an abstract class to simulate chemical reaction networks with Gillespie's \(\tau\) algorithm.

These aim to observe the number of molecules of \(\ell\) chemical species \(S_1,S_2,\dots,S_{\ell}\) that can react via \(K\) predefined chemical reactions \(R_1,R_2,\dots,R_K\) over a time interval \([0,T]\). This is basically done by simulating a discrete time Markov chain. More precisely, one subdivides the time interval into \(d\) smaller intervals of length \(au>0\). Let us denote the copy numbers at time @fj\tau \(, i.e., after the \)j \(th step by $\mathbf X_j$. Then, the system is updated via @f[ \mathbf{X}_{j+1} = \mathbf{X}_j + \cdot \mathbf{p}, @f] where \)\mathbf{S} \( denotes the \)\ell\times K \( stoichiometric matrix, whose entry at position \)(i,k) \( gives how many molecules of type \)@_fakenlS_i \( are created/lost by one reaction of type \)R_k \(; and where the vector \)\mathbf{p}=(p_1,p_2,\dots,p_K) \( contains \)K \( poisson variates simulating how often each reaction fires within the time interval \)[j\tau,(j+1)\tau) \(. The mean parameters of the poisson variate \)@_fakenlp_k \( are given by $a_k(\mathbf{X}_j)\tau$, where \)@_fakenla_k \( gives the propensity function of reaction \)@_fakenlR_k
